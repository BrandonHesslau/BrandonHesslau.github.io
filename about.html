About Me

Hi, I’m Brandon Hesslau, a software engineer specializing in real-time interactive systems, engine development, and gameplay programming.

I discovered programming in high school, and it quickly became both my career path and creative outlet. I earned my Computer Science degree from DePaul University in 2021, focusing on real-time systems and interactive technology.

During my final year, I co-founded Not a Sailor Studios and led engineering on our debut title Buddy Simulator 1984. I took the project from concept to full commercial release, building everything from gameplay systems to performance optimizations, and was thrilled to see it reach an enthusiastic global audience.

My technical toolkit includes C++, C#, Python, Java, and Lua, along with professional experience in Unity, Unreal Engine, and custom engine development. My work spans gameplay systems, simulation, performance optimization, and developer tooling.

Outside of work, I’m often experimenting with prototypes, designing developer tools, or exploring my own independent game projects.

If you’re looking for someone who can own a project from concept to delivery, I’d love to connect!
